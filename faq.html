FAQ



1.What is blood and plasma donation?
Ans.Blood and plasma donation is the process of voluntarily donating blood, plasma, or both to help people in need. The donated blood and plasma are separated into components like red blood cells, plasma proteins, and platelets. These components are then tested, stored, and used in medical procedures like blood transfusions and organ transplants.

2.Who can donate blood and plasma?
Ans.Anyone who is at least 18 years old, weighs at least 110 lbs (50 kg), and is in generally good health can donate blood. As for plasma donation, you must weigh at least 125 lbs (57 kg) and be at least 19 years old.

3.Where can I find blood and plasma donation centers?
Ans.You can find blood and plasma donation centers near you by visiting the website of the American Red Cross (https://www.redcrossblood.org/) or by using an online blood donation center locator (e.g., Blood Center Finder).
